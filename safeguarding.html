Safeguarding Statement

Last updated: 30 January 2026

DayTime Hub is committed to safeguarding and promoting the welfare of adults and young people who access our services, as well as our staff and volunteers. We take all concerns seriously and act promptly to keep people safe.

Our safeguarding commitments

  • We provide a safe, welcoming environment with clear boundaries and respectful conduct.
  • We follow safer recruitment practices, including role checks and DBS screening where required.
  • We train staff and volunteers on safeguarding responsibilities and how to report concerns.
  • We record and respond to safeguarding concerns promptly and appropriately.
  • We work with local agencies when a concern requires multi‑agency support or statutory reporting.

Safeguarding policies

We maintain the following policies and procedures (available on request):

  • Safeguarding Adults Policy
  • Safeguarding Children and Young People Policy
  • Safer Recruitment and DBS Policy
  • Code of Conduct for Staff and Volunteers
  • Whistleblowing Policy
  • Complaints Policy

Report a concern

If you have a safeguarding concern, please contact us as soon as possible. If you believe someone is in immediate danger, call 999.
